Research Question

Sustainability considerations:

A clear, well-defined research question guides efficient experimental design and minimizes unnecessary work, materials, and data collection. Beyond scientific relevance, formulating socially and environmentally responsible questions ensures that research contributes positively to both knowledge and society.
Considering the broader impact at this early stage helps align your project with principles of open, fair, and sustainable science.

Questions you could ask:

  • Does my research address a problem that has meaningful societal or environmental relevance?
  • Could the outcomes of my project contribute to more sustainable practices, policies, or technologies?
  • Can I design my study to generate insights that are reusable or that reduce future experimental burden?
  • Am I engaging stakeholders (e.g., clinicians, communities, or industry) to ensure the research question meets real-world needs?

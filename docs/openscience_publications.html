Sustainability considerations:

Publications are the main way research contributes to collective knowledge. Making your work openly accessible and transparently reported allows others to build upon it without unnecessary repetition â€” saving time, materials, and energy across the research ecosystem.
Sustainable publishing also means communicating your work responsibly: include sustainability considerations, share your data and code in FAIR (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable) formats, and highlight efficient or greener research practices.
Importantly, publishing negative or null results contributes to scientific honesty and prevents others from repeating unproductive experiments, thereby conserving both environmental and intellectual resources.
Choosing open-access journals and preprint servers, and being transparent about methods, further strengthens the sustainability and integrity of science.

Questions you could ask:

  • How can I integrate sustainability aspects (e.g., resource use, collaboration, open data) into my publications?
  • How can I make my manuscripts, datasets, and code openly available using FAIR principles?
  • Are there suitable venues or repositories for sharing negative or null results?
  • Which open-access publishing routes or institutional agreements can I use to minimize costs and maximize accessibility?
  • Can I include a short sustainability or reproducibility statement to inspire similar awareness in others?

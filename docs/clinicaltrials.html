Sustainability considerations:

Clinical trials are among the most resource-intensive research activities ‚Äî involving participant travel, clinical site operations, data management, and material use. Thoughtful planning can reduce environmental impact while maintaining scientific rigor, participant safety, and data quality.
Sustainable clinical research means designing studies that are adequately powered but not excessive, reusing data responsibly, coordinating across institutions, and minimizing unnecessary replication.
Digital tools (e.g., e-consent, remote monitoring, telemedicine) can also reduce travel and logistical burdens for both participants and researchers.

Questions you could ask:

  • Can my trial be designed to answer multiple research questions within a single protocol or cohort?
  • Am I collecting only the data truly necessary for my study‚Äôs aims, without excessive measurements?
  • How can I maximize insights within the given sample size (e.g., through multivariate or integrative analyses)?
  • Have I checked trial registries (e.g., ClinicalTrials.gov, WHO ICTRP) to ensure my study isn‚Äôt duplicating existing work?
  • Could digital or hybrid approaches (e.g., remote follow-up, wearable devices) help reduce travel and resource use?
  • Are there opportunities to share anonymized trial data responsibly to avoid future redundant studies

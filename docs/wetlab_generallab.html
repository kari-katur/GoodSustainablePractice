Sustainability considerations:

Wet labs are resource-intensive environments, consuming far more energy, water, and single-use materials than office-based research. While experimental work is essential, it can be performed in a more responsible and efficient way.
By planning experiments carefully, sharing resources, and integrating sustainable standard operating procedures (SOPs), researchers can significantly reduce environmental impact without compromising data quality, reproducibility, or safety.
Engaging your lab community in discussions about sustainability fosters shared responsibility and innovation in daily practices.

Questions you could ask:

  • How can I reduce single-use plastics, hazardous waste, and unnecessary energy consumption in my daily lab work?
  • Are there validated, more sustainable SOPs or equipment settings that could replace standard practices in our lab?
  • Can experiments be batched, shared, or scheduled to optimize equipment use (e.g., centrifuges, incubators, freezers)?
  • Are there tracking tools, dashboards, or internal initiatives to monitor and improve resource efficiency?
  • Have I discussed sustainability practices with my lab team or facility manager to identify collective improvements?

Sustainability considerations:

Computational research may seem low-impact, but data storage, analysis, and modeling can consume substantial energy ‚Äî especially when using high-performance computing clusters or training large models. Sustainable dry-lab practices aim to minimize this footprint through efficient coding, optimized workflows, and the responsible use of computational resources. Adopting green computing principles not only reduces emissions but often improves performance and cost efficiency. Collaboration with IT teams and awareness of institutional infrastructure can help you identify greener options for data analysis and storage.

Questions you could ask:

- What principles define green code, and how can I apply them in my analysis workflows?
- Who at my institute (e.g., IT department, HPC manager, data scientist) can advise me on optimizing my code or workflows for energy efficiency?
- Which computing cluster or storage option offers the best balance between performance, cost, and environmental impact?
- Can I measure or estimate the energy usage and carbon footprint of my computational tasks?
- Is there potential to reuse, share, or archive my data efficiently instead of duplicating large datasets?
- Could cloud or local computing solutions be optimized for sustainability (e.g., using renewable-powered data centers)?

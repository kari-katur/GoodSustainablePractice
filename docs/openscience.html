Sustainable and Responsible Open Science Practices

Open Science promotes transparency, accessibility, and collaboration in research, while Good Sustainable Practice ensures that these principles are applied responsibly and with long-term impact.
By integrating sustainability into Open Science, we aim to create research processes that are not only open and inclusive, but also ethically sound, environmentally conscious, and socially responsible.

Good Sustainable Practice in Open Science means designing research workflows that respect resources, support fair participation, and enable the reuse and longevity of knowledge.
This includes open access to publications and data, transparent methodologies, responsible data management, and practices that consider their societal and environmental implications.
Through these principles, Open Science becomes a driver for sustainable innovation, fostering trust, equity, and resilience in the global research ecosystem.

Open Circular Science

pen Circular Science extends Open Science by emphasizing reuse, longevity, and sustainability of research outputs.
It focuses on designing research processes and infrastructures that allow data, methods, and results to circulate responsibly, minimizing waste and maximizing societal value.

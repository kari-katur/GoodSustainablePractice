Collaborations

Sustainability considerations:

Open science and collaboration are key pillars of sustainable research. By sharing data, methods, and results transparently, we reduce unnecessary duplication of work, save time and resources, and accelerate scientific progress.
Collaborations enable access to shared infrastructure, complementary expertise, and distributed workloads ‚Äî all of which help lower the collective environmental footprint of research.
Embracing openness also strengthens equity and trust, ensuring that scientific knowledge benefits the broader community rather than remaining siloed within institutions.

Questions you could ask:

  • Who else is working on similar questions, and how can we join forces instead of duplicating efforts?
  • Am I sharing my data, code, and results in accessible repositories or platforms where others can reuse them?
  • Are there existing collaborations or networks I could join to pool resources or infrastructure?
  • How can open communication and transparent documentation improve the reproducibility and sustainability of my work?
  • What tools or institutional frameworks support open and collaborative research practices (e.g., OSF, Zenodo, GitHub, local data commons)?

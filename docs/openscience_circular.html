Strategies and Practices towards Openness

‚ÄúThe Open Circular Science approach is deliberately
based on the concept of the circular economy. It involves
the gradual decoupling of economic activity from the
consumption of finite resources and thus requires
systematic change.
- The basic principles of the circular economy can be
adapted for science:
- Keep data and results in use
- Design out: negative effects on the environment and climate
- Regeneration of natural systems‚Äù

Mission & Impact:
Define the research question,desired benefits, avoided harms, success criteria, and
how effects will be measured.
Openness:
Choose open practices, integrate them into the workflow, enable sharing and reuse, and clarify the
added value of strategic openness.
Sustainability:
Use existing infrastructures, procure sustainably, minimize resource and environmental
impacts, and reduce or recycle waste.
Resources & Conditions:
Identify needed skills and infrastructures, consider funder/institutional
requirements, use available support, and engage colleagues.

Mentorship

Sustainability considerations:

Strong mentorship is a cornerstone of sustainable research culture. Clear communication, structured supervision, and accessible guidance prevent avoidable mistakes, wasted materials, and unnecessary repetition ‚Äî all of which save time, energy, and resources in the long run.
A supportive mentorship environment also promotes well-being, collaboration, and knowledge sharing, helping early-career researchers grow into responsible and efficient scientists.
Sustainability in mentorship means building systems that value learning, transparency, and continuous feedback as much as productivity.

Questions you could ask:

  • Who is formally responsible for supervising and supporting my project?
  • Do I clearly understand the purpose and design of my experiments or analyses?
  • Have I received enough feedback to perform my work efficiently and reproducibly?
  • When is my next meeting with my supervisor or mentor ‚Äî and am I well prepared for it?
  • Are there opportunities for peer mentoring or lab-wide discussions to share expertise and reduce duplicated efforts?
  • How can I communicate challenges early to avoid unnecessary setbacks or wasted work?

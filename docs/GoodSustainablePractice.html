Why Sustainable Biomedical Research Matters

100 kg lab plastic per researcher / year

Health sector = 5th biggest global CO2 emitter

Lab energy use = 3 x typical office

Biomedical research is essential to improving health, but its environmental footprint is often overlooked.
From energy-intensive lab work to computational analysis and clinical logistics, research activities
generate significant CO₂ emissions, plastic waste, and energy demand.
This impact conflicts with the core mission of health sciences, especially as climate change increasingly
threatens public health. Yet, sustainability in life science research remains under-addressed—a 2022
review found only 25 publications on the topic, with none focusing on how better experimental
planning might reduce environmental harm.
As scientists, we have a responsibility to align our methods with our mission. By embedding
sustainability into research design, we can help protect both human and planetary health

Setting a New Standard

This initiative is more than an academic exercise—it’s a call to action. By demonstrating that high-impact
science can coexist with environmental stewardship, we aim to:

  • Provide concrete guidelines for sustainable research
  • Empower PIs and students to make informed, low-impact choices
  • Encourage collaboration, openness, and resource sharing
  • Advocate for institutional policies that support sustainable science

Practical Steps Towards Sustainable Research

Open Science - Share data / publications

Reuse - Data, protocols, reagents

Efficient Computing - Use low-impact servers

Smart Design - Avoid unnecessary experiments

Personal Responsibility - Ask: "Is this needed?"

Track Impact - Use tools to monitor your consumption

Emedding Sustainability in Biomedical Research

Smarter Planning, Lower Impact

We We hypothesize that strategic improvements in experimental planning—including dataset reuse, reduced redundancy, efficient
study design, and enhanced collaboration—can significantly reduce the energy consumption and overall environmental
footprint of biomedical research.By embedding sustainability into each stage of the research process, we can demonstrate
that scientific excellence and environmental responsibility are not mutually exclusive, but mutually reinforcing.
